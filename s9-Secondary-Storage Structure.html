Disk Structure

  • Disks provide the bulk of secondary storage for modern computer systems
  • Magnetic tape was used as an early secondary-storage medium
  • Although it is relatively permanent,and can hold large numbers of data, magnetic tape is slow
  • Magnetic tape is limited to sequential access
  • Tapes are currently used mainly for backup, for storage of infrequently used information, and as medium for tranferring information from one system to another

Disk Structure

  • Information on the disk is referenced by a multipart address, which includes the drive number, the surface, the track, and the sector
  • All the tracks on one drive that can be accessed without the heads being moved (the equivalent tracks on the different surfaces) constitute a cylinder

Disk structure

  • Disk Speed is composed of three parts
  • To access a block on disk, the system must first move the head to the appropriate track od cylinder
  • This head movement is called a seek
  • And the time to complete it is seek time
  • Once the head is at the right track, it must wait until the desired block rotates under the read-write head
  • This delay is latency time
  • Finally, the actual transfer of data between the disk and main memory can take place, this part is transfer time
